# Design Directions Scoring & Selection

We evaluate the 5 proposed design directions against the client's goal: "Modern, spacious, sans-serif, impactful. Less is more." 

The rubric criteria (scored 1-10):
1) **Offer clarity in first 10 seconds**: Is the main message immediately readable?
2) **Credibility / proof density**: Does the design naturally house logos, books, and quotes?
3) **Conversion path**: Is the CTA clear and actionable for event planners?
4) **Mobile polish**: Does it adapt gracefully without clutter?
5) **Speed discipline**: Is the layout lightweight and structurally simple?

### Top 3 Winning Directions & Rationale

**1. Editorial Momentum (Score: 46)**
*Why it won:* It perfectly answers the client's request for "Less is more". By utilizing massive negative space and oversized, beautiful typography, it creates an immediate atmosphere of premium authority. It naturally pushes proof elements (book covers, logos) into high-contrast zones.
*Focus:* Maximum contrast, stark layout, editorial typography hierarchy.

**2. Kinetic Clarity (Score: 46)**
*Why it won:* This direction focuses strictly on speed, conversion, and mobility. It relies on subtle grid layouts and micro-interactions, making it an excellent vehicle for clear, direct-response pathways. It performs best on mobile due to clean UI patterns and sticky CTAs.
*Focus:* Active user flow, tech-forward grid, bright and spacious.

**3. Structural Authority (Score: 45)**
*Why it won:* Taking a slightly more commanding (brutalist-lite) approach, this layout excels at proof density. By utilizing heavy, deliberate borders and robust color blocking, every testimonial and logo feels cemented into the page, projecting absolute resilience and strength.
*Focus:* Heavy borders, rigid grid, incredible proof density, bold and unapologetic.

*(Directions discarded: Starlight Confidence was deemed too heavy and potentially slow to render on mobile; Warm Precision leaned too casual/human for the "impactful" ask.)*
