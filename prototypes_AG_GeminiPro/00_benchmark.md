# Competitor Benchmark Extraction

**Brand Context:** Anne Grady Group represents resilience, leadership, and proactive adaptability. The goal is an impactful, modern, spacious, and sans-serif specific presentation that strongly out-executes the current corporate speaking landscape.

## Benchmark Analysis

| Competitor | Strengths | Weaknesses | Patterns Worth Matching | Patterns to Avoid |
|------------|-----------|------------|-------------------------|-------------------|
| **Simon T. Bailey** | Very bold asymmetrical layout, immediate authority. Great use of brand color (mustard yellow) as a structural block. | Feels somewhat blocky and rigid, lacking some dynamic white space. A bit uniform. | High-contrast hero section; stark, commanding display typography; sticky conversion path. | Monotonous grid blocks; overly dense inner paragraphs. |
| **Courtney Clark** | Exceptionally strong personal proof elements ("4-Time Cancer Survivor", "Brain Aneurysm Survivor"). Clear, emotional hooks. | The narrative leans almost fully into biography, slightly obscuring corporate outcomes. Text density on mobile can be high. | Immediate proof elements; named frameworks ("Resilience 3.0"). | Biography-first IA. Planners need outcomes first, biography second. |
| **Eileen McDargh** | Excellent trust architecture: features "Ranked 3rd in Communication" and strong, authentic client quotes. | Visuals and layout may feel slightly older/traditional compared to strictly modern "less is more" design. | Direct client testimonials integrated closely with the programs. | Generic "motivational speaker" copy and traditional hero sliders. |
| **Adam Markel** *(Industry baseline)* | High-production video assets, clear messaging, clear book positioning. | Often heavy on assets that slow down load times. | "What you get" structured outcomes; rich media usage. | Heavy JS-dependent page loads and layout shifts. |

## "Outbest" Targets for Anne Grady Group

1. **Faster Perceived Loading (Speed Discipline)**: Strict use of system/premium web sans-serif fonts, no forced heavy JS layouts, CSS-driven micro-interactions.
2. **Sharper Hero Clarity**: Moving away from the current site's generic "Adapt. Lead. Grow" over an image, delivering a stark, high-contrast, minimalist hero that forces attention to the primary offer (10-second test).
3. **Credibility Architecture (Proof Density)**: Rather than burying logos or testimonials, thread social proof (logos, book covers, short quotes) continually above the fold and between every major visual break.
4. **Superior Mobile Polish**: Implement a persistent, accessible "Book Anne" sticky CTA on mobile, massive tap targets, and typography that scales beautifully on narrow viewports.
5. **Outcomes-First IA**: Structure speaking topics not just by name, but strictly by *What the Audience Gets* to speak directly to the event planner's needs to derive ROI from the keynote.

## Design Theses

1. **Editorial Momentum**: A stark, magazine-like site that makes the speaker's authority feel undeniable, by using oversized sans-serif typography, massive negative space, and removing traditional corporate block layouts.
2. **Kinetic Clarity**: An energetic, tech-forward site that makes resilience feel active and modern, by using sharp grid lines, subtle CSS motion, and removing heavy, static background colors.
3. **Starlight Confidence (Dark)**: A premium, evening-event focused site that makes the keynote feel like an exclusive experience, by using deep dark-mode contrasts, glowing accents, and removing standard flat white backgrounds.
4. **Structural Authority**: A brutalist-lite, commanding site that makes the content feel rigorous and proven, by using heavy borders, high-contrast color blocking, and removing airy, disjointed elements.
5. **Warm Precision**: An approachable but highly polished site that makes resilience feel human and accessible, by using earthier neutral tones, softened geometry, and removing aggressive corporate framing.
